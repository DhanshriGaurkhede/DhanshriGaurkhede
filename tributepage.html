A Tribute to the God of Cricket: Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar, fondly known as "The God of Cricket," is an Indian former cricketer widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen of all time.

His career spanned over two decades, during which he set numerous records and inspired millions with his dedication, talent, and humility.

Key Highlights

  • Debut: 15 November 1989 (Test)
  • Last Match: 28 November 2013 (Test)
  • Runs Scored: 15,921 (Test), 18,426 (ODI)
  • Centuries: 51 (Test), 49 (ODI)
  • World Cup Wins: 1 (2011)
  • Awards: Bharat Ratna, Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy

Sachin's legacy extends far beyond the cricket field. He is a symbol of perseverance, determination, and sportsmanship, and his impact on the game and the nation is immeasurable.
